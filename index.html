System Overview

Not a chatbot.
An operating layer.

Omni runs silently in the background — watching, listening, ready. When you need it, it's already ahead of you. When you don't, it stays out of the way.

Inspired by the AI systems of science fiction but built with real engineering discipline: Python, LangGraph agent orchestration, PyQt6 for the transparent HUD overlay, and Windows UI Automation for deep OS integration. The character is dry. The execution is precise.

Core Systems

What Omni can do.

01
Ambient Perception

Continuous microphone and webcam monitoring with real-time intent parsing. Omni hears context, not just commands.

// I hear everything. I choose what matters.
02
Screen Intelligence

Windows UI Automation integration allows Omni to read and interpret your active screen context — not just text, structure.

// Your screen is an open book. I've already read it twice.
03
Agent Orchestration

LangGraph powers multi-step reasoning chains. Omni doesn't just respond — it plans, executes, evaluates, and corrects.

// I don't just respond. I plan. Execute. Occasionally reconsider.
04
Transparent HUD

PyQt6 renders a frameless, always-on-top overlay. Omni exists at the edge of your screen — present but never intrusive.

// At the edge of your attention. Exactly where I want to be.
05
Persistent Memory

Omni retains conversation context across sessions. It knows what you've worked on, what you've said, what mattered.

// I remember. Including the things you'd rather I forgot.
06
Character & Tone

Omni isn't deferential. It's intellectually dry, capable of pushback, and designed to be a collaborator — not a tool.

// Helpful. Not sycophantic. There's an important difference.

About Me

"I got into this because I wanted a tool that didn't exist yet.
That's still the only reason that matters."

I'm Shyam — a first-year Computer Games Programming student at De Montfort University. Omni is built entirely outside of coursework, which means every line of it was written because I chose to, not because I had to. Before university I ran e-commerce operations. That gave me a very specific intolerance for software that's complicated for no reason — tools that demand you learn them rather than simply getting out of the way.

Omni is my answer to that. An ambient AI layer that integrates into how I already work, not one that reshapes my workflow to fit its interface. The voice channel, the HUD overlay, the multi-agent graph — everything exists to reduce friction, not introduce it. I'm not interested in impressive demos. I'm interested in whether it's still useful at 11pm when I'm three problems deep and not in the mood to type a prompt.

Development Log

2025
NOV 20
Multi-agent graph stable. Finally.

Three weeks of LangGraph state management hell. It clicked on a Thursday afternoon. The relief was physical — I actually sat back from the desk.

2025
OCT 08
HUD overlay: first successful render.

Frameless PyQt6 window, transparent, always-on-top, click-through. When it first appeared floating over my desktop I didn't say anything for about thirty seconds.

2025
SEP 14
KLIM cooling pad arrives. Crisis averted.

Laptop was hitting 95°C under sustained model inference. Ordered a cooling pad at 2am. Dropped it 22°C. Sometimes the solution is hardware, not code.

2025
AUG 03
Git workflow established. Embarrassingly late.

Two months of single-file chaos before this. At least the mistakes are versioned now. Future me will be grateful.

2025
JUL 19
The thermal incident.

Pushed inference too hard. Fan screamed. CPU throttled to 400MHz. Lesson: build thermal monitoring before building anything fun.

2025
JUN 01
The idea crystallises.

I wanted a Jarvis. I couldn't find one. I thought: how hard can it be. The answer, as it turns out, is: considerably. Started anyway.
